Dr Isaac Newton

The maestro who revolutionalised science

Below is a timeline of Isaac Newton's life

  • 1642 - Born 25th December in Lincolnshire, England
  • 1661 - Admitted to Trinity College, Cambridge.
  • 1665 - Discovered Binomial Theorem and Calculus.
  • 1667 - Received his MA and became a Fellow of the College of the Holy and Undivided Trinity.
  • 1668 - Designed the first known functional reflecting telescope (Newtonian Telescope).
  • 1669 - Appointed Lucasian Professor of Mathematics.
  • 1672 - Appointed Fellow of the Royal Society.
  • 1687 His book Principia (Mathematical Principles of Natural Philosophy) was published.
  • 1689 & 1701 - Served as Member of Parliament for the University of Cambridge.
  • 1696 - AppointedWarden of the Royal Mint.
  • 1703 - Appointed President of the Royal Society and Associate of the French Academie des Sciences.
  • 1704 - His book Opticks, that explained the theory of colour was published.
  • 1713 - Second edition of Principia was published.
  • 1727 - Died 20th March in his sleep in London. He was never married!

"Newton was the greatest genius who ever lived, and was also the most fortunate, for we cannot find more than once a system of the world to establish."

-- Mathematician Joseph-Louis Lagrange
